Rethink Your Screen Time

An academic awareness project focused on how excessive phone use can affect student development, focus, mental health, and real-life social interaction.

Project focus

Why this issue matters

This project asks students to think critically about how often they use their phones and what that use may be replacing. Rather than only focusing on rules or restrictions, the project emphasizes awareness, reflection, and healthier habits.

6 years
A commonly cited estimate suggests that by age 17, teens may spend years of their lives looking at screens.
Play-based → Phone-based
Jonathan Haidt argues that childhood has shifted away from real-world play and toward screen-based interaction.
Awareness first
The goal is to help students recognize their own habits and make more thoughtful choices about screen time.
Book connection

The Anxious Generation

This reform project was shaped by Jonathan Haidt’s argument that young people need a wide variety of real-world social experiences in order to develop into flexible and socially skilled adults. The project connects that idea to student life by asking how much time is now being spent on phones instead.

The project is not only about reducing screen time. It is about creating more room for focus, face-to-face interaction, and reflection.
Key questions

What this project asks

  • How much time are students actually spending on their phones each day?
  • How does that affect focus, sleep, and social development?
  • Can awareness change everyday habits?
  • What would students do with more time off-screen?
